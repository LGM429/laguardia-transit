Community Reviews Port Authority's LaGuardia Mass Transit Proposal

By Leo Miranda

Oct. 5th, 2023

The Port Authority of New York and New Jersey (PANYNJ) presented a LaGuardia mass transit proposal to the Queens Community Board 1 in September. The PANYNJ has already authorized further planning and design of the project to the tune of $30 million. The meeting aimed to inform and field questions from the Queens community.

The proposal's main features include improved service to the LaGuardia Link Q70 shuttle service and a new non-stop shuttle service from end of the N/W line at Ditmars Avenue in Astoria to the airport.

The project has an onus on getting Manhattan-LaGuardia commuters “out of cars and onto mass transit,” as Hersh Parek, Director of Government and Community Relations, said. “Nearly half of customers that fly out of LaGuardia are coming from Manhattan.” The PANYNJ scrapped earlier plans for an air train service—in the style of JFK airport—due to cost and construction limitations.

In parts of Queens and the South Bronx, pollution is an issue of equal access to a healthy environment. Sections of Astoria are known as "Asthma Alley,” due to the elevated pollution levels from automobiles and power generation nearby.

Studies have shown higher rates of asthma in low-income neighborhoods adjacent to major transportation areas. Efforts to get cars off the road by providing clean transit alternatives may be part of New York's response to pollution and the climate crisis.

The estimated cost of the Port Authority transit project is $500 million and projects to serve five million riders annually. The full report can be downloaded here.
